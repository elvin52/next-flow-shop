What Are Hijab Styles?

Hijab styles refer to various ways Muslim women wrap and style their head coverings. People often use "hijab and scarf" as the same thing; however, a hijab is more than a scarfâ€”it symbolizes modesty and cultural identity. Modern hijab styles range from simple daily wraps to detailed layered looks using quality fabrics and accessories.

1. Classic Wrap Hijab

Easy
Difficulty

Styling Time

Step-by-Step Technique:

1

Position the hijab on your head with one side hanging longer (2/3) than the other (1/3)

2

Take the longer side and wrap it around your face under your chin

3

Bring the wrapped side up over the opposite shoulder

4

Pin securely under your chin for stability

5

Drape the remaining fabric over your shoulder and pin at the shoulder

6

Adjust the fabric around your face for comfort and complete coverage

Frequently Asked Questions

Which hijab style suits my face shape?

Based on my 12+ years as a hijabi and styling clients, oval faces can wear any style. Round faces look great with Turkish and Al-Amira styles. Square faces benefit from soft Classic Wrap. Heart-shaped faces suit styles with bottom volume.

How do I style a hijab without pins?

Use jersey hijabs for grip, hijab magnets for invisible securing, or instant hijabs with built-in caps.

What's best for hot weather?

Choose lightweight chiffon or modal. Classic wrap and Al-Amira provide airflow. Use breathable cotton undercaps.

How do I prevent slipping?

Use undercap for grip, choose textured jersey fabric, use magnets strategically, ensure proper wrapping.
